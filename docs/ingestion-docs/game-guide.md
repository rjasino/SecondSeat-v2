# Leon A

Resident Evil 2 Remake is - as the title suggests - a complete overhaul of (at the time of writing) a twenty one year old game. The bad stuff - beyond clunky controls, fixed camera angles - has been removed but the other idiosyncrasies that characterise the series, such as the item box and healing herbs that can be combined to enhance their effectiveness, are present and correct. The (very) shiny game engine really enhances the level design and the game is, despite the dark aesthetic, astonishingly beautiful at times.

## Gameplay

Despite its reputation as a horror game, the game is fundamentally an action adventure with environmental puzzles. A major part of the challenge is resource management. You generally have somewhere between not quite enough and nowhere near enough ammunition to deal with all the enemies that threaten you. You have to choose what to carry, what not to carry and what to leave where it is. The gunplay is excellent but that's not really where it's at - enemies are tough and you are quite squishy and very much outnumbered. Instead, part of the challenge is choosing when to fight and when to run: if you insist on blowing every enemy away, at some point you will find yourself stuck.
There are two protagonists and four scenarios. One character's "A" scenario and the other character's "B" scenario form a pair. The other pair of scenarios form an alternative timeline. This provides excellent replayability for what is a short game: your first playthrough may be eight hours but your fourth will be more like three.

## Difficulty Levels

There are three selectable difficulty levels: Assisted, Standard and Hardcore.
Assisted difficulty gives you weaker enemies and aim assist. If your health level is reduced to the Danger level, it will automatically regenerate to the Caution level after a minute-or-so. You can take quite a few hits before you see the game-over screen.
Standard difficulty presents, in my opinion, a decently robust challenge. You will have to manage resources and avoid damage but you should rarely feel like you're being masochistic playing the game. You can typically take two hits and survive, although some attacks will take you straight into the Danger zone.
Hardcore difficulty pits you against significantly stronger and more aggressive enemies. The resource issues you face on Standard difficulty are compounded by having less inventory space (a maximum of 14 slots rather than 20). Most attacks will take you straight into the Danger zone. In addition, your ability to save is restricted since you have to use Ink Ribbons which are a limited resource. When I played the game on Hardcore difficulty, I found that there were enough ribbons in the A scenarios for this not to be too much of an issue. The B scenarios give you rather fewer of them and there were sections where I cursed the inability to save. Besides rationing your savegames, Hardcore difficulty removes checkpointing. In my opinion, this is a mistake - repeatedly playing the same difficult section is the antithesis of fun.
Besides the difficulty level that you have selected, the game features a hidden dynamic difficulty. If you're struggling in a section (for example, you've died several times), hidden adjustments will be made so that your weapons do a bit more damage and enemy attacks do a bit less. Similarly, if you're dancing around enemies, the difficulty may be adjusted slightly upwards.

If you manage to get an S-Rank on Standard difficulty, you will unlock a Samurai Edge pistol with infinite ammo that you can use for all future playthroughs. If you're good enough to get an S-rank on Hardcore difficulty, you will unlock a bottomless SMG, the LE 5.
The end of scenario report also lists how many times you saved. If you achieve the time required for S-rank and saved three times or less, your rank will go up to S+. Getting S+ rank on Hardcore will unlock extremely powerful bonus weapons: the Gatling Gun for Claire's scenarios and the ATM-4 rocket launcher for Leon's. Note that using an infinite weapon such as the Samurai Edge or LE 5 will lock you out of the S+ rank. Which means, basically, that being able to unlock the Gatling Gun or ATM-4 means that you don't actually need it!

## Health Management

Your current health is colour-coded. If you are in the green, your health is above 67%. If you are in the red, it is 30% or below. Between 30% and 67%, your health meter is amber. So long as you are not in the red, you can only be killed by instant death attacks (such as from an Ivy). If your health is red, on the other hand, you are typically one hit from death and should do something about it.
A single Green Herb restores 33% of your health, but you should never use just one. Instead, you should generally combine herbs: G+R fully restores health while G+R+B fully restores health and halves incoming damage for a few minutes. A Blue Herb on its own will cure you of the poison status (inflicted by G-Adults) but will not restore health so you should generally use it in combination. If you're going for the Frugalist trophy, note that an R+B Herb gives you damage protection without invalidating the trophy.
You should generally carry a full health restore at all times to guard against things going awry. If you are going into an area where there's little danger and can pick up health restoratives along the way, I will tell you in the walkthrough and you can leave restorative items behind.

## Resource Management

All ammo is precious but some ammo is more precious than other ammo. By and large, MAG Ammo and SMG Ammo should be reserved for boss encounters. Claire's Flame Rounds can only be found and not crafted and should be used against the tougher non-boss enemies like Lickers, G-Adults and Ivy Zombies. Leon's shotgun, on the other hand, is rather ineffective against these same enemies and can instead be used to conserve handgun ammo when fighting zombies. Flamethrower fuel, on the other hand, does work against the enemies that shotgun shells are ineffective against and should be conserved accordingly.
In the B scenarios, Large-Caliber Ammo is somewhat limited and you'll generally be running low. Use your Combat Knife against enemies on the ground and craft 9mm ammo to make up the shortfall.

## Time Management

Since there are bonuses for completing scenarios in as short a time as possible, it is best to avoid wasting time. The most effective method is to run everywhere. If you die, quit to the title screen to load your most recent save. Otherwise, the timer doesn't get reset.
It is quite straightforward to S-rank all four scenarios on standard difficulty while picking up all goodies so long as you run everywhere and have a good idea of where to go next. You can use my in-depth S-rank guide to help. If you want to get the S-rank on Hardcore difficulty, you are going to have to leave a bunch of stuff unclaimed:

- Spare Parts for the Safety Deposit Room are absolutely optional. The ultimate reward (a Hip Pouch) is good, but you have to do a lot to get it. If you choose to skip this, you don't have to unlock the Linen Room or open Portable Safes. In the B scenarios, you can avoid the Observation Room / Interrogation Room and in Leon's scenarios, you don't have to pick up the Diamond Key either.
- The police cruiser in the Parking Garage contains a so-so accessory for Leon and an indifferent weapon for Claire. If you choose not to bother, you don't have to go into the Firing Range either.
- Developing the "Hiding Place" film and picking up the loot is very much optional.
- If you're really Hardcore, you can avoid using the Safety Deposit Room altogether since the Shotgun / Grenade Launcher will be found in the Umbrella Laboratory if you don't pick it up in the Police Station.
- In the A-scenarios, the Nap Room Hip Pouch requires significant backtracking. This is not such an issue in the B-scenarios.
- In the B-scenarios, you can create a more efficient path through the first part of the game by going to the S.T.A.R.S. Office earlier. The critical path would be something like Operations Room -> Spade Key -> S.T.A.R.S. Office -> Lounge -> Library -> West Storage Room -> Library -> Landing -> Goddess Statue. Since you'll be chased by Mr. X and ganged up on in the Library, this will require luck and in all likelihood a G+R+B Herb to survive. Using the lift in the Safe Room despawns Mr. X so you can pick up stuff afterwards.

## Equipment

### Leon

#### Matilda

This is Leon's starting weapon and holds 12 rounds of 9mm ammo. It can be upgraded with three accessories:

- High Capacity Magazine
  - Found in the puzzle desk in the West Office. This expands the capacity to 24.
- Gun Stock
  - Found in the boot of the police cruiser in the Parking Garage. This enables a 3-round burst fire mode. I prefer not to use it because it burns through ammo and makes the pistol take up two inventory slots.
- Muzzle Brake
  - Found in the safe in the Waiting Room. This makes the gun more accurate and reduces recoil.

#### W-870

This is a high-powered shotgun found in the Weapons Locker in the Safety Deposit room. It has a capacity of 4 shells. A point-blank headshot will kill a zombie. It can be upgraded with two accessories:

- Long Barrel
  - Found in Gun Shop Kendo after escaping from the police station. This expands the capacity to 8, reduces spread and increases range. It makes the shotgun take up two inventory slots. Despite this, it is worth using.
- Shotgun Stock
  - Found in the safe in the Water Treatment Room. It increases firing speed and reduces loading time (and makes your shotgun look like a shotgun).

#### Lightning Eagle

This is a high powered pistol found in the Armory in the S.T.A.R.S. Office. It has a capacity of 7 rounds and uses the very rare .50 caliber ammo. As with the shotgun, it can kill a zombie with a single round and can do so from range. It can be upgraded with two accessories:

- Long Barrel
  - Found in a box in the basement area of the police station that can be unlocked using the S.T.A.R.S. Badge. This increases the damage but makes the gun take up two inventory slots.
- Red Dot Sight
  - Found in Wesker's desk in the S.T.A.R.S. Office after developing the Hiding Place film.

#### Chemical Flamethrower

This is found in the Sewer Storage Room where you pick up the King and Queen Plugs. It takes up two inventory slots. Its capacity is 400 units of fuel which it gets through at a fair-old rate. It panics non-undead enemies and is the only one of Leon's weapons that can kill Ivies permanently. It can be upgraded with one accessory:

- Regulator
  - Found in a locker in the Nap Room in NEST. This improves fuel efficiency.

#### Anti-Tank Rocket

This given to Leon during the fight against the Super Tyrant. In the Leon's B scenario you can also use it in the G-5 fight. It has just four rockets. Which is plenty.

#### M19

This is the pistol that Leon obtains in the Guard Room at the start of the B scenario. It holds 7 rounds of .45 ammo and cannot be upgraded.

### Claire

#### SLS 60

This is Claire's starting pistol and holds 5 rounds of 9mm ammo (despite being a .38 Special in all but name). It can be upgraded with two accessories:

- Speed Loader
  - Found in the puzzle desk in the West Office. This improves the otherwise horrible reload speed by allowing all 5 rounds to be reloaded in a single action.
- Reinforced Frame
  - Found in the safe in the Water Treatment Room. This allows the SLS 60 to be loaded with .357 ammo (the same damage as Leon's Lightning Hawk pistol). Unfortunately, the ammo is extremely rare and cannot be made.

#### GM 79

This is a grenade launcher found in the Weapons Locker in the Safety Deposit room. It only holds a single grenade and has a lengthy reload time. The Flame Rounds are extremely powerful and effective against the toughest non-boss enemies in the game: Lickers, G-Adults and Ivy Zombies. Unfortunately, they cannot be made. The Acid Rounds, which can be made, are less awesome. Despite stunning the Birkin boss in all his incarnations, the stun period seems to be less (as far as I can tell) than the reload time. It can be upgraded with one accessory:

- Shoulder Stock
  - Found in a locker behind a Diamond door in the police station basement. This allows the launcher to be aimed directly but makes it take up two inventory slots.

#### JMB Hp3

This is a pistol found in the boot of the police cruiser in the Parking Garage. It holds 13 rounds of 9mm ammo and has a red dot that replaces the normal aiming reticule. I personally find the red dot to be less effective than the normal reticule which means that I prefer the SLS 60. It can be upgraded with one accessory:

- High-Capacity Magazine
  - Found in the safe in the Waiting Room. This increases the ammo capacity to 26.

#### MQ 11

This is a submachine gun found in the Armory in the S.T.A.R.S. Office. It has a capacity of 24 rounds and its ammo is fairly rare. Each shot has fairly low damage but the high rate of fire makes its DPS extremely high. It can be upgraded with two accessories:

- Suppressor
  - Found in a box in the basement area of the police station that can be unlocked using the S.T.A.R.S. Badge. This greatly reduces the recoil but makes the gun take up two inventory slots and is necessary to apply sustained damage to a target.
- High-Capacity Magazine
  - Found in Wesker's desk in the S.T.A.R.S. Office after developing the Hiding Place film. This increases the ammo capacity to 50 rounds.

#### Spark Shot

This is a stun gun found in the Sewer Storage Room where you pick up the King and Queen Plugs. It takes up two inventory slots. You can charge up the shot by holding down the L2 button after shooting which makes it very powerful - it can one-shot Lickers (but, curiously, not zombies). It can be upgraded with one accessory:

- Voltage Condenser
  - Found in a locker in the Nap Room in NEST. This increases damage.

#### Minigun

This is found just before the G-4 boss fight and has a flat 400 rounds. Which you may run out of.

#### Quickdraw Army

This is the pistol that Claire obtains in the Guard Room at the start of the B scenario. It holds 6 rounds of .45 ammo and cannot be upgraded. It fires extremely quickly but the reload time is extreme.

### Secondary Weapons

#### Combat Knife

Simple and dependable. It wears out quickly when used to counterattack but slowly when used to slash enemies on the ground.

#### Flash Grenade

Does no damage but can stun any enemy (or groups of enemies).

#### Hand Grenade

Does huge damage to groups of enemies (and outright kills zombies within its blast radius). It's rather wasted if used as a counterattack, since then it will only damage a single foe.

### Bonus Weapons

#### Infinite Combat Knife

This is the reward for destroying all 15 Mr. Raccoon figures. it behaves like the standard Combat Knife except it doesn't break. If used to counterattack, however, it must be retrieved. This makes it not so good if used against enemies that you simply want to get past (G-Adults, for example). I found that if you use it in the Birkin boss fights, it can be lost permanently.

#### Samurai Edge (Original Model)

This is the reward for obtaining an S-rank on Standard difficulty. It is a handgun with a fixed 15 round capacity. It cannot be upgraded (so does less damage than Matilda or the SLS 60) but does not require you to carry ammo for it. This means that you can clear hallways without having to worry about running out of ammo. Note that if you use this, you cannot obtain S+ rank.

#### LE 5

This is the reward for obtaining an S-rank on Hardcore difficulty. It is a submachine gun with a fixed 32 round capacity. It cannot be upgraded (which means it is less effective than the fully upgraded MQ 11) but since it does not require you to carry ammo for it, you can murder pretty much anything. Note that if you use this, you cannot obtain S+ rank.

#### ATM-4

This is the reward for obtaining an S+ rank on Hardcore difficulty in either of Leon's scenarios. It's a rocket launcher with infinite ammo which can one-shot pretty much any enemy in the game. Since S+ rank precludes using any other infinite weapon, unlocking this means you don't really need it.

#### Gatling Gun

This is the reward for obtaining an S+ rank on Hardcore difficulty in either of Claire's scenarios. It's like the Minigun but with infinite ammo. Since S+ rank precludes using any other infinite weapon, unlocking this means you don't really need it.
