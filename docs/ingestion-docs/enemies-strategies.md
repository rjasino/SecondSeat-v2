# Leon A

## T-Virus Zombie

These are your basic "grunt" enemies. You can kill them absolutely by getting a critical headshot that turns their skulls to a mush. However, you only need to kill zombies if they're somewhere that you're going to come back to repeatedly or they're unavoidably blocking somewhere you need to go. Otherwise, you can shoot them in the head to stagger them and run past. Smart players shoot them in the knees to amputate a lower leg, leaving them crawling on the ground and easy to avoid now and later. When they're on the ground, you can use a combat knife to stab them to a second and permanent death. If you drop them, they often play dead and will grab you as you go past. Try slashing them with a combat knife to check that they're really dead. If you absolutely must kill one right now, a point-blank shotgun blast or a magnum round to the skull will do the trick. Note that it game terms, they are supposed to be simple obstacles, therefore spending time and ammo blowing their distinctly bullet-spongey skulls to pulp is maybe not the optimum strategy.

## Licker

This is the second enemy type you encounter but fortunately you encounter them rarely. These are like mini-bosses who can absorb a lot of damage and dish a terrific amount out. Their tongue attack is surprisingly long distance and their pounce attack is pretty much guaranteed to take your health into the danger zone. They are blind and track you by sound which means that you can sneak past them if you walk very slowly. However, these things are often placed somewhere where they're a serious and repeated obstacle and there's typically something else that will attack you when you're trying to be very discreet. They are uniquely vulnerable to Flash Grenades, which make them deaf as well as blind, and fire, which Claire gets access to long before Leon.

## Zombie Dog

Fortunately, you only encounter these pests in a couple of places. They are very fast and very dodgy. While they go down to three or four well-placed pistol shots, the optimal strategy if you can manage it is simply to put a door between yourself and them.

## Tyrant (Mr. X) (Lurker)

For Leon A Hardcore, the biggest mistake players make with Mr. X is treating him like an enemy to defeat. He's actually a time-management and route-planning problem.

### Don't Shoot Mr. X

Unless you're trapped, shooting him is usually a waste of ammo. You can stagger him temporarily, but he'll come back and Hardcore ammo is far more valuable against zombies, lickers, and bosses.

### Clear Key Hallways Before He Appears

The best time to kill zombies is before Mr. X starts stalking the RPD.
Priority hallways: Main Hall routes, West Office corridor, Library, STARS Office corridor, Weapons Locker hallway
The real danger isn't Mr. X himself. It's Mr. X + zombies + lickers at the same time. Veteran players often recommend clearing these frequently used routes early.

### Learn to Walk

Mr. X is attracted by gunshots, running and other loud noises. If you hear distant footsteps, stop sprinting unnecessarily. Walk when possible and only run when relocating quickly.

### Listen for Footsteps

Your ears are more important than your eyes. Loud footsteps means same floor and nearby, muffled footsteps means different floor and chase music means he has visual contact. Often you can determine his location without ever seeing him.

### Abuse Safe Rooms

Mr. X cannot enter several rooms in the RPD. Notable safe locations are STARS Office, Dark Room, Clock Tower, Secret Room beneath the Goddess Statue and most save rooms (except the Main Hall)

### Use the Library to Juke Him

The Library is one of the easiest places to lose Mr. X. Common tactics are Let him come toward you, go around a bookshelf or ladder, drop down or circle around, and Exit through another door. Because he's large and turns slowly, you can often run circles around him. Community players frequently use the Library for this.

### Carry One Flash Grenade

For Hardcore, I consider this mandatory. If Mr. X corners you or a licker is nearby or a zombies block the hallway, a flash grenade can save a run instantly. Many experienced players save flashes specifically for Tyrant encounters.

### Bait the Punch

When he's directly in front of you run toward him, wait for the punch wind-up, move to his side and run past. His attacks have a noticeable recovery animation. Once you get comfortable, you'll realize he's much less dangerous than he looks.
